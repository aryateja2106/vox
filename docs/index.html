The Problem

The terminal is the most powerful tool on your computer. And the most intimidating.

You know what you want to do. You just don't remember the exact syntax. Is it find -mtime or find -newer? Is the flag -r or -R? Does kill take a port or a PID?

Today, you either Google it — breaking your flow — ask ChatGPT and pay by the token, or wade through man pages written for the 1970s.

Every senior engineer has a cheat sheet. Every terminal power user has muscle memory built over years. That knowledge shouldn't be locked behind experience.

What if your terminal just... understood you?

How It Works

Three steps. That's it.

Step 01

You speak

Type — or say — what you want in plain English. No special syntax. No command structure. Just describe the outcome.

Step 02

Vox translates

A local model under 1B parameters converts your words to the exact shell command for your system. Runs entirely on-device.

Step 03

You decide

Review the command before anything runs. Hit Y to execute, N to skip, or C to copy it to your clipboard. Always in control.

Why Vox

Built differently. On purpose.

🔒

100% Local

Runs entirely on your machine. Nothing leaves your terminal. No telemetry, no cloud calls, no one watching your commands.

⚡

Tiny Model

Under 1B parameters. Fits on any laptop with room to spare. No discrete GPU needed — runs on CPU.

📦

One Curl Install

A single curl | bash and you're done. No npm, no Docker, no creating yet another account.

🏠

Platform Smart

Detects macOS vs Linux automatically. Gives you the right flag, the right tool, for your exact system.

🎤

Voice Ready

Built for voice input from day one. Talk to your terminal the way you'd describe a task to a colleague.

🔗

Open Source

MIT licensed. The model weights are public. Fork it, retrain it, embed it in your own tool. It's yours.

Usage

Three ways to use it.

# Interactive REPL — keep it open, keep asking
vox
 
# One-shot — translate a single request
vox "find all log files from today"
 
# Auto-execute — skip the prompt, run immediately
vox -x "show free disk space"
